THE PROBLEM

People try to make money in their spare time in the stock market. Many of them are not professional stock traders or have no background in finance. But many stock trading website is not beginner friendly because of old UI designs with redundant information and options. Beginners are easy to get overwhelmed by the design of the websites itself. For example, Scottrade and Fidelity have very complicated UX process and have long submenus for each dropdown.

THE SOLUTION

Bounce solves the problems of stocking trading for beginners.The stock trading website I want to design will be very beginner friendly with selected information and options.

MY ROLE

I functioned as the UX researcher, UX designer, visual designer for this project. Within these roles, I conducted user research, created wireframes, conducted user interview, and completed visual designs.

THE RESEARCH

User Research

I designed a user research survey to know my target users. My questionnaire focused on:

  • How many times do they trade stock every year;
  • How many years of experience do they have;
  • What frustration they have with their current broker;
  • What do they think their broker can be improved;
  • What information they would like to get from a stock trading website.

Results were gathered from 27 respondents who are interested in stock trading across the country.

Those who are not interested in stocking trading are excluded from the survey by the first question, so only datas of those 26 respondents who are interested in stock trading are collected. I summarized the datas and figured out my target audiences, their frustrations and needs.

  • (1)93% of who are interested in stock trading ranges from 18 ~ 45 yrs. Since most of the users ranger from 18~45 yrs, we can make a trendy design which may be appealing to investors
  • (2)Only 38% are happy with their current brokers, 62% think their brokers can be improve in some ways, 26% think they are very difficult to use (See Question 5)
  • (3)27% feel frustrated when they miss companies important events (See Question 5)
  • (4)They want simple design for beginners, easier money transfer from bank, organized stock transactions(See Question 6)
  • (5)They want to about a company’s earning/revenue growth, stock type(dividend type, growth type etc.), cash flow(See Question 8)
  • (6)They want an important event calendar with alerts, price change notification, filters for stock selection(See Question 9)

Personas

I conducted one-on-one interviews with three of my survey respondents to find out more details about their responses and help us understand the problem better. From these interviews, I developed three personas and identified there are three major user groups:

  • Caro represents pet owners who live in small towns where limited pet hotels are available;
  • Tara represents pet owners who have limited incomes like students, they prefer cheaper cost for pet services;
  • Max represents pet owners who live in a costly area like San Francisco, they like the idea of credit exchange for pet services.

The demand for reliable and cheaper pet service is popular. I also identified some features for implementation in the future development process, such as adding filters for senior dog experience, puppy experience, rescue dog experience, etc.

Competitive Analysis

After having a comprehensive understanding of my target audiences and what they need, I started to do SWOT analysis for three popular stock trading brokers (Robinhood, Interactive Broker, Fidelity) in the market. And I examined their UI/UX designs in details by testing out these websites myself and reading app reviews.

Screenshots of Competitors

User Stories

Based on all the surveys, interviews and competitive analysis, I came up with a list of user stories. While making a list of user stories, I only pick those features and information that audience want the most based on the survey. And those features that users doesn’t want (Like news feed, comment are, a list of stock invested by great investors) I didn’t put it on the user stories list. So that I can make a simple design for the website.

User Flows & Sitemap

I converted user stories into user flowsand sitemap to visualize the navigation process and content hierarchy. In this part, I not only referred to the competitors’ UX design process and also referred to some websites that provide stock resource, including SimpleWallStreet and other financial websites like Barron’s, Seeking Alpha.





BRANDING

Many logos of pet’s brands can be divided into two groups: (1) colorful pet image to display a happy pet (2) one color strokes to present a feeling of clean and simple. From my research, I concluded that this product needed to get a balance between these two. Because the cuteness of pets can attract users, and the cleanness and simple can make users feel reliable.

Typography

Righteous is an open sans typeface. Its round bowl, counter, and aperture create a feeling of decor and cuteness. It is suitable for headlines and reminds people of the cuteness of puppies.

Gudea’s sans serif displays a simple, condensed structure that brings a pleasant feeling when used at any size. Gudea was initially used in engineering documents. It also provides a sense of reliability.



Brand Story & Logo

The word “puppyface” came up when I was browsing some dog’s facebook pages. Puppy with cute and smiling faces usually gets a lot more “likes.” People’s heart is melty when they see a puppy. When I first walked my dog in the community as he was a puppy, he drew a lot of attention from my neighbors like a superstar. It is hard for people to ignore puppies’ innocent and adorable faces.

PuppyFace needed a logo to promote its public recognition and personality. The logo design begun with sketches of various puppy faces and evolved into a simple illustration of a puppies face with round eyes, nose, pink tongue and two big ears. Round eyes, nose, and pink tongue display cute personality. Dropped ears present relaxation. Shaded yellow color conveys a feeling of reliability and stable. So the logo itself transmits a message of cuteness and loyalty.


Colors

PuppyFace needs a color palette to trigger people’s feeling for pets. Bright green and bright orange are chosen to present pet’s happiness. The orange color reminds people of dog’s activeness, and green color reminds people of dogs playing on grass. The color inspiration came from playing with my dog in a park.

EXECUTION

Paper Prototype

I started paper prototype by quickly sketching the structures. While I was outlining, I focus on providing information and features pet owners concerned the most. Paper prototype allowed me to quickly create different layouts for sitter list, profile, reservations. I conducted simple preference testing using paper prototype. From the feedback, I learned that users prefer information in a row rather than in columns. The “z” eye tracking theory can explain this result well.
