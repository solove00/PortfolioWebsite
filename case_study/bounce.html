Overview

Problem

People try to invest in the stock market in their spare time. Many of them are not professional stock traders or don’t have a finance background. Many stock trading websites are not beginner friendly because of outdated UI designs with redundant information and options. Beginners are easy to get overwhelmed by the design of the websites itself. For example, Scottrade and Fidelity have very complicated UX process and have long submenus for each dropdown. They provide too much information including news, market trends, etc., which users can also find from many other news sources, not the trading website.

Solution

Bounce solves the problems of complicated stocking trading for beginners. The stock trading website I designed is very beginner friendly with information and options specifically curated for beginners. I carried out series of surveys and usability tests to find out the information that is needed for beginners and the best design help them trade easily.

Research

User Research

I designed a user research survey to know my target users. My questionnaire focused on:

  • How many times do they trade stock every year;
  • How many years of experience do they have;
  • What frustrations they have with their current brokers;
  • What do they think their brokers can improve;
  • What information they would like to get from a stock trading website;
  • What features are important to them.

Results were gathered from 27 respondents who are interested in stock trading across the country.

Those who are not interested in stocking trading were excluded from the survey by the first question, so only data of those 27 respondents who are interested in stock trading were collected. I summarized the data and figured out my target audiences, their frustrations, and needs.

  • 93% of who are interested in stock trading ranges from 18 to 45 yrs old. Since most of the users range from 18 to 45 yrs old, we can make a trendy design which may be appealing to them.
  • Only 38% are happy with their current brokers. 62% think their brokers can improve in some ways. 26% think they are very difficult to use (See Question 5).
  • 27% feel frustrated when they miss companies' important events (See Question 5).
  • They want a simple design for beginners, easier money transfer from the bank, organized stock transaction history (See Question 6).
  • They want to know about a company’s earnings/revenue growth, stock type(dividend type, growth type, etc.), cash flow (See Question 8).
  • They want price change notification, filters for stock selection (See Question 9).

Personas

I conducted video interviews with three of my survey respondents to know more about their trading background and lifestyle. From these interviews, I developed three personas and identified there are three major user groups:

  • Michael represents stock traders who are long term investors and believe company financials are very important;
  • Tara represents stock traders who know very few about the stock market and want some guides;
  • Max represents stock traders who think current brokers are difficult to use, and he needs something simple.

Competitive Analysis

After having a comprehensive understanding of my target audiences and what they need, I started to do SWOT analysis for three popular stock trading brokers (Robinhood, Interactive Broker, Fidelity) in the market. And I examined their UI/UX designs in details by testing out these websites myself and reading website reviews.

User Stories

Based on all the surveys, interviews and competitive analysis, I came up with a list of user stories. While making a list of user stories, I only picked features and information that users want the most based on the survey. And those features that users don’t want (Like news feed, comment area, a list of stock invested by great investors) I didn’t put it on the user stories list. But these features can be saved for future development of the website. For now, I just made a simple design and will continue to do more usability tests and add some necessary features.

User Flows & Sitemap

I converted user stories into user flows and sitemap to visualize the navigation process and content hierarchy. In this part, I not only referred to the competitors’ UX design process and also referred to some websites that provide the stock resource, including Simply Wall St and other financial websites like Barron’s, Seeking Alpha.

Branding

Typography

Bounce needs two simple typefaces to make the design and content easy to read. Noto Sans and Arimo are both sans-serif typeface. They are simple and they both give the design a modern look when compared to a serif typeface.

Noto Sans was designed to help make the website more beautiful. It's "little boxes" style will give the website a harmonious look and feel. Arimo was designed to display an innovative, refreshing feeling. It also offers improved on-screen readability which is good for content text. Both typefaces have a neutral feel and can work in a variety style.

Brand Story & Logo

“Never try to time the market and nobody has ever done this successfully and consistently”, the saying is a golden rule for stock trading. That’s because the market itself is bouncing up and down and nobody can catch the lowest point and highest point consistently. So going up and down is the natural and normal behavior of the stock market. So here comes the name “Bounce” for my stock trading website. Bounce itself reflects the attribute and movement of stocks. And this attribute is embedded into many people’s mind. If you ask people what they think of the stock market, they may say it is like a rollercoaster.

Bounce needs a logo to promote its public recognition and personality. The logo design began with various sketches of letter “B” combined with arrows and evolved into a simple illustration of an arrow. The arrow itself looks like a letter “B”. It indicates positively that the stock market is going up. The green color in the stock market means gain. So it helps to fortify the feeling of making money in the stock market.

Colors

Navy blue was chosen as the main color for its association with security and trust. The white color was chosen to balance dark background. Grey is chosen as the color for texts and some inactive icons. Light blue was the pop color for buttons. Green and red are standard colors for the stock market. They were used to highlight some data or charts.

Execution

Paper Prototype

I did a paper prototype for the entire website including many features. I chose to do that because the paper prototype is very helpful from my experience. It helps to save a lot of time in the later development process. I can just use the paper prototype as a reference for creating low fidelity wireframe.

A simple user test was done to test the navigation and contents. The user was asked to finish the following tasks, and he provided a lot of good suggestions in the 40 minutes video.

The user was asked to finish the following tasks:

  • Log in/Sign up
  • Buy 20 shares Apple stock at the market price
  • Buy 20 shares Apple stocks at a limit price
  • Buy 10 shares Applied Material stocks at a limit price
  • How to find a stock that meets your requirements
  • How to set notifications
  • Where is your profile information and how to change?
  • How to transfer money
  • Where to find information of all money and stocks you have?
  • Where to find tax documents and account statements?

According to the feedbacks and analysis of the usability test, I came up with a list of potential changes that I can do to improve the website:

  • Only show the last four digits of users’ social security numbers
  • Change the wording of “go to homepage” to “start trading” or “let’s make some money”
  • Use radio button design for “Order Type” and “Expiration Type”
  • Change “balance” to “cash balance”
  • Highlight price in order reviewing section
  • Move stock price section above trade section
  • Change the wording of “filter” to “stock picker” or “stock screener”
  • Remove “share price” section on stock page
  • Add dividend section to stock picker
  • Replace infinite symbol with the largest market cap
  • Add a sorting feature for stock picker results
  • Customize notifications for each stock
  • Remove the summary section under account

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

I created low fidelity wireframes through Sketch to simulate the desired interaction flow and feel of the product. The purpose of the low fidelity wireframe test is to check if the design has the right navigation and if people can find the right content and finish task easily. I created clickable low fidelity prototype by InVision and using Usertest.io to finish the test. I required the test website to recruit five users between 18~45 years old who are interested in stock trading. The reason why I only recruit people between 18~45 is that according to the survey at the beginning of the project I already knew that these people are the majority population of stock trading beginners.

The whole usability testing process consists of seven tasks and three questions. They are listed below:

  • Imagine you are a stock trading beginner, you don't have an account, please sign up.
  • Please purchase 20 shares of Apple stock at market price.
  • Imagine you own AMAT (Applied Material) stock, please sell 10 shares at a limit price ($53.00).
  • You would like to turn on/off notifications (such as price change/big events/new product release) for all stocks on the watchlist. How would you do it?
  • Imagine you want to find a stock that meets certain requirements (such as growth rate, earnings), can you find it and add it to your watchlist?
  • If you want to know whether a stock is overvalued/undervalued, where would you find this information?
  • Please find out where to transfer money to/from banks.
  • What made you frustrated when using the website?
  • What's good about this website?
  • What can we do to improve the website?

I watched recorded videos of the user’s behaviors over and over again to make sure I catch every detail, at the same time, I observed and wrote down the reactions of users. Here are 10 pages detailed analysis of five usability tests. Based on users’ feedbacks and my analysis, there are potential changes to the website:

  • Add wire transfer option
  • Stress bank account safety
  • Change the wording of search bar
  • Change the wording of review button
  • Add explanations/descriptions
  • Add instant response after a market order
  • Re-design the user flow for selling stocks
  • Increase the difference between a buy box and a sell box
  • Indicate what’s the next information to fill out
  • Re-design user flow for notification settings
  • Consolidating some notification options
  • Improve feedbacks after stocks are added
  • Change the wording of “industry”
  • Options for users to know more about value calculation
  • Add drop-down menu
  • Simplified company introduction
  • Allow users to jump to certain content on the stock page
  • Focus on beginner use and prevent overkill by providing tips and description
  • Remove home tab and move search bar to the right
  • Add summary section to to account

Key Learnings

During the process of creating this website, I was challenged by the breadth and depth of stock trading knowledge and users’ demand for a simple and clean website providing adequate information for beginners. Working on this project taught me the importance of learning as a designer. If a designer is making a design for an unfamiliar industry, it is very important that the designer gains professional knowledge to make a good design.

With more time, I would consider doing user research for two potential features which were mentioned by users during of usability test in later product development process: (1)Compare users’ portfolio performance to S&P 500 or Nasdaq; (2)During the buying process, users can input the amount of money they would like to spend instead of shares of stocks they want to buy. I think these two features are great and they will probably enhance the user experience of the website. But I need to do research and tests to confirm my assumptions.
