ABOUT BLOCBOX

Cloud service is becoming more and more important nowadays. In the past, we stored information on removable disks and personal computers, but now it is a trend to use cloud services to store files. It is safer, offers more capacity and is more accessible. Blocbox is a cloud storage and file-sync tool. It lets us store all file types including notes, images, and links.


THE PROBLEM

Even though there are many cloud service apps in the marketplace, many of the current apps are lacking in a good user experience. My research found that users prefer a simple design with less features. Too many features sometimes is a problem.

THE SOLUTION

I conducted user research, user stories and flows to better understand users’ needs and create a file saving app that has an intuitive user experience.Here is a link to the partially coded site: https://solove00.github.io/Blocbox/, and the link to Invision Prototype : https://projects.invisionapp.com/share/QYDOADQWS#/screens. In order to provide the best user experience, I conducted UX research to find out what features were most valuable, and what layout users prefered.


THE RESEARCH

User Research

I designed a user research survey to know my target users. My questionnaire focused on:

  • How many times do they trade stock every year;
  • How many years of experience do they have;
  • What frustrations they have with their current brokers;
  • What do they think their broker can be improved;
  • What information they would like to get from a stock trading website.
  • What features are important to them

Results were gathered from 27 respondents who are interested in stock trading across the country.

Those who are not interested in stocking trading were excluded from the survey by the first question, so only data of those 26 respondents who are interested in stock trading were collected. I summarized the datas and figured out my target audiences, their frustrations and needs.

  • 93% of who are interested in stock trading ranges from 18 ~ 45 yrs. Since most of the users ranger from 18~45 yrs, we can make a trendy design which may be appealing to investors
  • Only 38% are happy with their current brokers, 62% think their brokers can be improve in some ways, 26% think they are very difficult to use (See Question 5)
  • 27% feel frustrated when they miss companies important events (See Question 5)
  • They want simple design for beginners, easier money transfer from bank, organized stock transactions(See Question 6)
  • They want to about a company’s earning/revenue growth, stock type(dividend type, growth type etc.), cash flow(See Question 8)
  • They want an important event calendar with alerts, price change notification, filters for stock selection(See Question 9)

Personas

I conducted video interviews with three of my survey respondents to know more about their trading background and lifestyle. From these interviews, I developed three personas and identified there are three major user groups:

  • Michael represents stock traders who want to know company financials and big events;
  • Tara represents stock traders who know very few about stock market and want simplified user interface.
  • Max represents stock traders who think current brokers are difficult to use, and he need something simple;

The demand for simple and easy design for stock trading beginner is popular. I also identified some features for implementation in the future development process, such as stock picker based on market cap, earning growth, revenue, etc.

Competitive Analysis

After having a comprehensive understanding of my target audiences and what they need, I started to do SWOT analysis for three popular stock trading brokers (Robinhood, Interactive Broker, Fidelity) in the market. And I examined their UI/UX designs in details by testing out these websites myself and reading app reviews.

User Stories

Based on all the surveys, interviews and competitive analysis, I came up with a list of user stories. While making a list of user stories, I only pick those features and information that audience want the most based on the survey. And those features that users doesn’t want (Like news feed, comment area, a list of stock invested by great investors) I didn’t put it on the user stories list. But these features can be saved for future development of the website. For now I just made a simple design and will continue to do more usability tests and add some necessary features.

User Flows & Sitemap

I converted user stories into user flows and sitemap to visualize the navigation process and content hierarchy. In this part, I not only referred to the competitors’ UX design process and also referred to some websites that provide stock resource, including Simply Wall St and other financial websites like Barron’s, Seeking Alpha.





BRANDING

Typography

Bounce needs two simple typefaces to make the design and content easy to read. Didact Gothic and Arimo are both sans-serif typeface. They are simple and they both give the design a modern look when compared to serif typeface.

Didact Gothic was designed to present each letter in the form most often used in elementary classrooms. It works as a simple headline font that feature a simple outline style. Arimo was designed to display an innovative, refreshing feeling. It also offers improved on-screen readability which is good for content text. Both typefaces have a neutral feel and can work in a variety style.



Logo & Colors

Blue was chosen as the main color for its association with security and trust. Black and grey were chosen as the colors for texts. Purple is the pop color for buttons. The high saturation of purple color will draw user’s attention easily. Green and red are standard colors for stock market. They will be used to highlight some data or charts.
